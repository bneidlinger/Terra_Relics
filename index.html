Frequently Questioned

Is this real?

Philosophically, that's above our pay grade. Commercially, yes. We will absolutely ship you organic matter in a sealed bag. Whether that constitutes "real" is between you and your epistemology.

What's actually in the bag?

Leaves. Soil. The occasional twig. Each bag is hand-curated from locations we describe with more poetry than precision. Contents may vary. Meaning will too.

Can I open it?

Technically, yes. But opening the bag collapses the narrative waveform. The relic transforms from "priceless artifact of a dying world" to "dirt." We recommend keeping Schrödinger's compost sealed.

Is this tax-deductible?

We're not accountants. We're artists. That said, several customers have successfully categorized their purchase as "professional development," "conceptual research materials," and "therapy."

What if I'm disappointed?

Disappointment is a feature, not a bug. It means you expected something. That expectation—that hope—is what we're really selling. No refunds, but we do offer free validation.

Why does this exist?

Because capital follows narrative, and we wanted to see what would happen if we said that out loud. Also, because the forest had extra leaves and we believe in reducing waste through recontextualization.
